eFP-Seq Browser

Welcome to the eFP-Seq Browser. A new web-app by the University of Toronto's BAR labs which allows you to anlyze RNA-Seq data of Arabidopsis thaliana with tissue localization and statistical information

What is it?

The eFP-Seq browser shows mapping coverage of RNA-Seq data across multiple localized tissue using an electronic fluorescent pictographic (eFP) images that uses colours to visually represent expression levels of a particular gene under a particular growth condition and developmental stages.

What is eFP?


Electronic fluorescent pictographic, or eFP for short, are pictographs of tissues from an organism that allows an individual to determine visual representations of experimental data.

The numbers? What do they mean?

The eFP-Seq Browser provides statistical information on the analyzed RNA-Seq data by using two statistical analysis technicals called RPKM (Reads per Kilobase per Million reads mapped) and the Pearson Correlation Coefficient(PCC) to sort unbiased relative comparisons

It is as easy as using a micropipette

Using the eFP-Seq Browser for your own data is so simple that it is just a click away. Having your RNA-Seq BAM files put on a cloud service such as Amazon AWS or Google Drive, you can have your data analyzed and visualized using the eFP-Seq Browser.
