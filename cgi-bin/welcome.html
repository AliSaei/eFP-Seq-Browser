eFP-Seq Browser

Welcome to the eFP-Seq Browser. A new web-app by the University of Toronto's BAR labs which allows you to analyze RNA-Seq data from Arabidopsis thaliana with tissue localization and statistical information

What is it?

The eFP-Seq Browser shows mapping coverage of RNA-seq data across multiple samples using an electronic fluorescent pictographs (eFPs) that use a colour scale to visually represent expression levels of a particular gene under a particular growth condition and developmental stages, based on the mapping coverage, as RPKM units.

What is eFP?


Electronic fluorescent pictographs, or eFPs for short, are pictographs of tissues from an organism onto which quantitative experimental data can be projected.

The numbers: what do they mean?

The eFP-Seq Browser provides statistical information for any desired gene for a given RNA-seq data set in the form of RPKM (Reads per Kilobase per Million reads mapped), which is a summarization of the gene's expression level, and the Pearson Correlation Coefficient (PCC), which is a measure of the gene's read map similarity to the chosen gene model. In this way, strong expressors can be sorted to the top of the list, or read maps that most closely match the gene model can be identified simply by sorting on that column.

It is as easy as using a micropipette

Using the eFP-Seq Browser for your own data is so simple that it is just a click away. Having your RNA-Seq BAM files put on a cloud service such as Amazon AWS or Google Drive, you can have your data analyzed and visualized using the eFP-Seq Browser.
